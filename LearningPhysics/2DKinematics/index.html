Projectile motion describes an object that is thrown, or shot, in the presence of a gravity field. Thus, the acceleration of gravity is the only acceleration acting on the projectile object. Because the vertical acceleration of gravity is the only acceleration, the horizontal motion remains at a constant velocity. Also, in these problems, it is only the vertical motion that determines the time the object is in the air, not the horizontal motion.
