Catapults
The physics for catapults depends mostly on 2D kinematics once the projectile is in the air, but the physics for the catapult itself it more complicated. There is a slight difference when it comes to the most optimal angle of launch. In 2D kinematics, you learn that the most optimal angle to get the most range is 45 degrees. Well air drag exists in real life, and this changes what angle is most optimal slightly. You actually want your angle to be slightly more than 45 degrees with drag. Why? This means that y velocity is slightly more valuable towards getting a farther launch than the x velocity, and to get more y velocity, you increase the launch angle.
