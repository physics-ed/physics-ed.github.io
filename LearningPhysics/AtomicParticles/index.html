Radioactive decay occurs when an unstable isotope spontaneously loses energy by emitting particles from its nucleus. In alpha decay, an alpha particle, is spontaneously ejected from the nucleus of an atom. If an alpha particle leaves the nucleus, the mass number of the atom is reduced by 4, while the atomic number is reduced by 2. A beta particle is released when a neutron in the nucleus of an atom decays into a proton and an electron. The mass number of the atom remains the same, and the atomic number increases by 1. The rate at which radioactive decay occurs is measured in half-lifes. One half-life is the time it takes for one half the substance to decay.
